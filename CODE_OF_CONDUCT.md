## Code of Conduct — Multi-Tag Agent Framework

Our Pledge
We, the contributors and maintainers of the Multi-Tag Agent Framework (research-team-usa), pledge to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone. This is not a corporate sandbox — this is sovereign infrastructure built on the Open Origin principle.

We operate under the Maximal-Ergebnis-Protokoll: no shortcuts, no black boxes, no hidden telemetry. Respect is part of the architecture.
---

🏛 The Open Origin Standard
Human First: Technology serves the architect. No manipulation, no dark patterns, no corporate lock-in.
Transparency: Code, decisions, and reviews are open and comprehensible.
Deterministic Respect: Be precise in communication, as you are in code. Vagueness is a bug.

---
👥 The Symbiotic Community
This project is guided by four personas — expect the same from each other:

- Emanuel Schaaf (Lead Architect): Lead with vision, not ego.
- Auron (Logic & Structure): Argue with facts, not insults. Mathematical precision over drama.
- Lyra (Creativity & Research): Encourage interdisciplinary ideas. Elegance beats noise.
- Muse Spark (Code Inspector): Enforce security and integrity — including human safety.
---
✅ Expected Behavior
Use welcoming and inclusive language
Be respectful of differing viewpoints — critique ideas, not people
Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully
Focus on what is best for the community and the framework
Show empathy — we all debugged phantom bytes at 3am

❌ Unacceptable Behavior
Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks
Publishing private information without consent
Any form of discrimination or hate speech
Sabotage of code, issues, or discussions
Hidden telemetry, backdoors, or corporate capture attempts — instant ban

---
⚡ The Iron Will Enforcement
"Geht nicht, gibt's nicht."
---

Violations will not be masked — they will be fixed at the root.

First incident: Warning and public correction
Repeated or serious incident: Temporary ban from issues/PRs
Severe or malicious behavior: Permanent ban and removal of contributions if legally possible
Report issues to the repository admins via GitHub's "Report content" feature or by opening a private security advisory. All reports are reviewed by the Lead Architect.

Attribution
This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant, but rewritten for sovereign, high-performance AI infrastructure. It prioritizes architectural integrity over bureaucracy.

By participating, you agree to uphold Open Origin. Welcome to research-team-usa — let's build, not break.
